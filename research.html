RESEARCH INTERESTS



I am interested in the support structures existing in the areas of information science and the visual arts - those things which frame, present, and construct and those which guide, organize, and facilitate.



My area of interest is software, programming languages and related media with historical significance in the domain of the visual arts. I am interested in the languages used by designers and artists for computer art, computational media design, and those developed for teaching general programming concepts to artists and designers with no prior programming experience.



As part of my graduate studies I conducted scholarly inquiry and service evaluation in the field of library and information science. I designed and planned a research study from conceptualization to proposal writing using scientific research methods and used evidence-based practice to support decision making. Along with developing an overview and understanding of social science research methods, I broadened my understanding of Digital Humanities research and Digital Scholarship Services offered within academic libraries.

The research area is digital scholarship services within academic libraries and the research topic is competency requirements for staff within academic libraries offering digital scholarship services to humanities researchers. This topic relates to my personal background and professional experience. I come from a humanities background in the visual arts and have a strong interest in the digital tools in use and service needs of DH researchers.
